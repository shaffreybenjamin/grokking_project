================================================================================
1. ONE-PARAGRAPH PROJECT CONTEXT
================================================================================

Modular addition mod p=113 with two architectures: a single-layer single-head
transformer (Nanda/Poncini setup, d_model=128) and a one-hidden-layer ReLU MLP
with separate left/right embeddings (Chughtai setup, d_hidden=512). Both are
trained to grok (test_acc=1.0) with matched hyperparameters. The static
analysis (phase1_belief_geometry.ipynb) replicates Poncini (PIBBSS report) on
the converged models: fit (α_k, ω_k) Fourier frequencies from logits, decode
EHMM predictive vectors at residual-stream positions, control against an
HMM-simplex baseline. The *developmental* phase tracks the formation of these
representations across all saved checkpoints (~160 log-spaced epochs) — a
quantitative replacement for staring at per-frequency plots at every step.

================================================================================
3. WHAT TRAJECTORY.PY ALREADY DOES (DON'T DUPLICATE)
================================================================================

`trajectory.py` is the lighter-weight developmental tool. For each checkpoint:
  - Decode the EHMM polygon at a chosen position (POS4 or M2 by default; POS3
    or M1 also supported), using (ω_k, α_k) fitted ONCE at the final checkpoint
    and held fixed across the sweep.
  - Decode the HMM-simplex one-hot control at the same position.
  - Record both R² values per checkpoint.

Position-appropriate EHMM target (already correct in trajectory.py):
  POS3, M1 → ⟨v(a)| + ⟨v(b)|     (additive, pre-modular-sum)
  POS4, M2 → ⟨v(a+b mod p)|      (single-circle, post-modular-sum)
  POS1, POS2 → ⟨v(a)| or ⟨v(b)|  (single-index)

A subtle config detail: ridge_alpha=1e-3 for EHMM fit, but simplex_alpha=1.0
for the HMM-simplex fit. The simplex target has p=113 one-hot columns and
gets ill-conditioned at early checkpoints — alpha=1.0 stabilises that fit
without changing the converged values (they agree to ~0.005 with alpha=1e-3).
DO NOT change this without a reason.

Outputs of `sweep_transformer` / `sweep_mlp`:
  epochs, r2_ehmm, r2_simplex, omegas, alphas, position, p, run_name, ...

Plotting helpers: `plot_trajectory`, `plot_trajectory_comparison`. Both
support `clip_negative=True` to handle rare ill-conditioned R² < 0 spikes.


================================================================================
4. WHAT DEVELOPMENTAL.PY IS MEANT TO DO (THE NEW WORK)
================================================================================

Whereas trajectory.py answers "how much of the *eventually-learned* algorithm
is present at each checkpoint", developmental.py answers "how complex / how
formed is the algorithm at each checkpoint". For each checkpoint, freshly
fit (ω_k, α_k) and record several scalar diagnostics:

    n_eff            number of frequencies needed for the logit fit's
                     cumulative R² to reach target_r2
    n_dominant       number of frequencies with α² > 10% of the largest α²
                     (threshold-free w.r.t. R² convergence)
    logit_r2         cumulative R² achieved at this checkpoint
    alpha_entropy    Shannon entropy of the normalised α² distribution
                     (low = concentrated; high = spread)
    per_freq_r2      supervised R² at the chosen position for each of the
                     top-K fitted frequencies, decoded individually
    r2_ehmm          supervised R² of the full EHMM at the chosen position
    r2_simplex       supervised R² of the simplex one-hot control

Plus plotters:
    plot_developmental_signature  4-panel stack (logit-fit R², N_eff,
                                  α² entropy, top-K per-freq R²)
    plot_structure_formation      single-panel R²(EHMM)/R²(simplex)/N_eff
                                  with a vertical line at the first epoch
                                  where structure has formed
    detect_structure_formation    decision function returning the first
                                  formation epoch + a boolean mask

Current state of developmental.py: scaffolded with sweep_developmental_*,
_step_metrics, detect_structure_formation, plot_developmental_signature,
plot_structure_formation. *n_dominant is NOT yet implemented*; the docstring
mentions it but the code doesn't compute it. THIS IS THE FIRST TODO.

5. KEY FINDINGS FROM THE STATIC PHASE THAT THE DEVELOPMENTAL PHASE INHERITS
================================================================================

5.1 The transformer uses ~4 dominant Fourier modes
    Final-checkpoint fit gives ω = (49, 30, 25, 41) with large α²; lower-α
    frequencies (15, 10, 23, 53, 38, 34) are SPURIOUS HARMONICS arising from
    cos(ω_i)·cos(ω_j) products in the MLP. Diagnostic:
        49 + 30 = 79, and 113 − 79 = 34   ✓
        49 + 41 = 90, and 113 − 90 = 23   ✓
    These spurious modes have supervised R² ≈ 0.5 at POS4 — they're recovered
    only as cos-components, not as full (cos, sin) pairs, hence half-recovered.
    THE ALGORITHM IS 4-FREQUENCY. Spurious harmonics are not "extra structure"
    the model learned, they're arithmetic byproducts of the multiplication.

5.2 The cos-only Poncini basis caps R² below 1.0
    The Poncini ansatz z = Σ α² cos(2π ω (a+b−c)/p) only fits the part of z
    that's SYMMETRIC under u → −u (since cos is even). A real trained model's
    logits have a small asymmetric (sin-of-u) residual. For our transformer:
        R² with 4 freqs   ≈ 0.97
        R² with 20 freqs  ≈ 0.9878   (asymptote)
    Adding more cosine frequencies doesn't help — the residual lives in sine
    directions the basis can't reach. Implication: target_r2=0.99 is
    UNREACHABLE for our model. If you set target_r2=0.99 and max_freqs=K,
    n_eff will saturate at K at every checkpoint — useless as a signal.
    Use target_r2=0.95 (which IS reachable, around 4 frequencies) for the
    developmental sweep.

5.3 The MLP spreads its representation across ~10+ frequencies
    Final-checkpoint fit gives ω = (22, 33, 41, 16, 51, 7, 36, 27, 19, 48)
    with similar α² across all 10. Per-frequency supervised R² at M1 is
    1.000 for every fitted ω; at M2 it's uniformly ~0.85. This is Chughtai's
    weak universality — the MLP doesn't concentrate like the transformer.
    Implication: the n_dominant metric will read very different numbers for
    the two architectures (transformer ~4, MLP ~10+). That's a real finding
    to surface, not an artifact.

5.4 Choice of decoding position matters
    POS4 (transformer) and M2 (MLP) are both *immediately before unembed*,
    where activations encode prediction-confidence info (the simplex one-hot
    of c is decodable). For the MLP this inflates the simplex R² to ~0.69
    at convergence and confounds the EHMM-vs-HMM separation. POS3 (transformer
    pre-MLP) and M1 (MLP pre-ReLU) are cleaner because the modular sum hasn't
    been computed yet — simplex R² stays near zero.

    The trajectory.py default is post-MLP (POS4/M2). For developmental
    analysis, decide deliberately:
      - Post-MLP: matches Poncini's main result, sharp grokking shape for
        transformer, but conflates "EHMM" and "prediction-collapse" for MLP.
      - Pre-MLP: cleaner cross-architecture comparison, but you're tracking
        the additive structure ⟨v(a)|+⟨v(b)| rather than the post-sum
        polygon. Both stories matter.

5.5 The MLP's d_hidden=512 dilutes the per-frequency PCA recovery
    With d_hidden=512 and 10 frequencies of similar amplitude, the top 10 PCs
    don't isolate any single frequency — they're linear combinations across
    multiple ω. n_pcs=10 in semi-supervised decoding gives R²≈0.1 per
    frequency at M1, even though SUPERVISED decoding at M1 hits R²=1.0 (the
    information IS there, just not in the top 10 PCs). Bumping n_pcs to ~40
    captures the full Fourier subspace.

    For the developmental sweep this means: per-frequency supervised R² is
    the right metric to track (full activations, no PCA), and is what
    developmental.py uses. PCA-based per-frequency R² would be much noisier
    at the MLP and shouldn't be used as a developmental signal.

5.6 Static MLP is being retrained at d_hidden=256
    There's an open option to retrain the MLP at d_hidden=256 (matches
    Chughtai exactly and concentrates the representation). If that retrain
    happens, the developmental sweep should be re-run on those checkpoints —
    it'll likely show fewer dominant frequencies and cleaner per-frequency
    R² trajectories.


================================================================================
6. DECISIONS ALREADY MADE (DON'T RE-DEBATE)
================================================================================

  - target_r2=0.95 (NOT 0.99) for the developmental sweep — model can't reach
    0.99 with the cos basis (5.2).
  - max_freqs=10 (NOT 20) for the developmental sweep — modest headroom over
    the ~4 dominant transformer frequencies, matches the user's intuition,
    avoids the asymmetric-residual asymptote issue.
  - n_dominant is the headline metric (threshold-free); n_eff is a secondary
    diagnostic (tied to a target_r2 choice).
  - n_dominant uses 10% threshold (α² > 0.10 · max α²) by default. The
    threshold is arbitrary but the *trajectory* of n_dominant across training
    is the signal, not its absolute value.
  - per_freq_r2 uses SUPERVISED decoding (full activations), not PCA→ridge
    semi-supervised — the latter is unreliable at d_hidden=512 (5.5).
  - The structure-formation detector triggers when ALL of:
        r2_ehmm > 0.95 AND r2_simplex < 0.20 AND n_dominant ≤ small_int
    Default `small_int = 10` is loose; tighten if false positives appear.

================================================================================
9. INFRASTRUCTURE NOTES
================================================================================

  - Each developmental sweep takes ~3–5 minutes per architecture (~160
    checkpoints, one forward + one fit + ~6 ridge regressions per checkpoint).
    Run with `verbose=True` to see a tqdm bar.
  - GPU is preferred but not required — sweeps complete in reasonable time
    on CPU (~5 min instead of ~3).
  - Memory: a single (p², d_model) activation matrix is ~12k × 128 × 8 bytes
    ≈ 12 MB, trivially fits.
  - The supervised-decode ridge fit at the simplex (113 columns) issues
    LinAlgWarnings at very-untrained checkpoints. Set warnings.filterwarnings
    or pass simplex_alpha=1.0 (already the default). Both EHMM and simplex
    R² values are clipped to ≥ 0 in plot_trajectory / plot_structure_formation
    via clip_negative=True.
  - Recommend running sweeps in a separate notebook cell (not interactively)
    so you don't accidentally rerun the slow part while iterating on plotting.
